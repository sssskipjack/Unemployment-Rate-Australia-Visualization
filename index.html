Exploring Employment and Unemployment Trends in Australia

This page visualizes Australia's unemployment trends over time and across regions, offering a closer look at how employment rates vary over the years.

Employment is one of the most important indicators of a nation's economic health. This interactive time series chart presents a historical overview of unemployment rates in Australia over the past five decades. By exploring these trends, we can see how global economic crises, local policies, and societal changes have shaped the Australian labor market. Unemployment rates vary widely across different states, reflecting the diverse nature of Australia's economy. Here, you can explore this fluctuation and uncover which states have been most affected by economic events, like recessions, and which have maintained relative stability. Use the scroller to zoom into specific periods to discover how unemployment impacts different parts of the country over time.

Behind unemployment statistics lie numerous personal and structural factors that vary across age groups. This sunburst chart offers insight into the reasons behind unemployment in Australia by breaking them down across different demographics. Younger workers may face difficulty finding their first job, while older workers might struggle due to changing industries or health issues. These reasons, from educational mismatches to broader economic shifts, create an intricate web that helps explain why people remain unemployed and what challenges they face when trying to enter the workforce.

Unemployment is just one part of the employment spectrum. In this stacked area chart, we explore how Australians move between different employment categories. By analyzing these flows over time, we can better understand how certain economic conditions lead to increases in unemployment. This visualization provides a clearer picture of the dynamics of the labor market and how people navigate the complexities of employment.

Geography plays a significant role in shaping employment opportunities. This choropleth map highlights the variation in unemployment rates across Australia's states and territories. The darker shades represent areas with higher unemployment rates, while lighter shades represent more stable regions. Viewing these trends geographically reveals how economic activities, local industries, and regional policies affect job availability.
