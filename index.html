Exploring Employment and Unemployment Trends in Australia

This page visualizes Australia's unemployment trends over time and across regions, offering a closer look at how employment rates vary over the years.

This interactive time series chart offers a comprehensive view of the unemployment rate fluctuations across Australian states over the past 50 years. Use the scroller to zoom into specific timeframes and compare state-level unemployment trends, revealing insights into the Australian labor market's evolution over time.

This sunburst chart breaks down the reasons for unemployment across different age groups, offering deeper insight into the factors influencing unemployment across various age ranges. The outer sections delve into more specific reasons for unemployment.

Stacked Area Chart: This chart visualizes various employment categories such as employed, discouraged job seekers, and active job seekers over time, allowing for a more in-depth analysis of the flow between different employment statuses.

Choropleth Map: A geographic perspective on unemployment rates across Australia. Darker shades represent higher unemployment, with users able to view trends across different years.

References

The data for this visualization comes from the Australian Bureau of Statistics: ABS Labour Statistics
