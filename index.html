Exploring Employment and Unemployment Trends in Australia across the years.

This page visualizes Australia's unemployment trends over time and across regions, offering a closer look at how employment rates vary over the years.

This interactive time series chart offers a comprehensive view of the unemployment rate fluctuations across Australian states over the past 50 years, revealing the impact of key events such as the 1983 Recession, The Global Financial Crisis, and the COVID19 Pandemic. Use the scroller to zoom into specific timeframes and compare state level unemployment trends, to see critical insights into how the Australian labor market evolves over time.

This sunburst chartk breaks down the reasons for unemployment across different age groups. Inner sections highlight broader categories and for that respective category, the outer sections delves into more specific reasons explaining the reason for unemployment. This chart offers a deeper understanding of the factors which influence unemployment across the various age ranges, adding a human element to the broader labor trends.

Stacked Area Chart - Employment Metrics Over Time: The stacked area chart visualizes the composition of various employment categories such as employed, discouraged job seekers, and active job seekers overtime. This allows you to see trends not only in unemployment but also in broader labor market participation. It illustrates the flow between different employment statuses and how major events shifted people between being employed, unemployed, or not potential workers

This choropleth map provides a geographic perspective on unemployment, highlighting regional disparities in Australia's unemployment rates. Darker shades represent higher unemployment, allowing for a clear visual distinction between areas with more robust labor markets and those experiencing economic difficulties. The map is dynamic, with users able to select different years to see how unemployment distribution has changed over time, particularly around events like the 1990s Recession or the COVID19 pandemic
