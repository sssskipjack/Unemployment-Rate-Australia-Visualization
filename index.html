Australia's Employment Landscape: Regional and Temporal Trends

This dashboard visualizes Australia's unemployment trends over time and across regions, offering a closer look at how employment rates vary geographically and historically.

The choropleth map above highlights the unemployment rates across Australia's states and territories for the selected year. Darker colors represent higher unemployment rates, offering a clear contrast between regions with more challenging labor markets and those with better employment conditions.

This interactive time series chart offers a comprehensive view of the unemployment rate fluctuations across Australian states over the past 50 years, revealing the impact of key events such as the 1983 Recession, The Global Financial Crisis, and the COVID-19 Pandemic. Use the scroller to zoom into specific timeframes and compare state-level unemployment trends, to see critical insights into how the Australian labor market evolves over time.
